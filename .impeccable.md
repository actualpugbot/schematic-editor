## Design Context

### Users
Minecraft builders, map makers, server admins, and redstone or structure creators who need to inspect uploaded schematic files before building, sharing, or debugging them. They are usually comparing shape, height, block distribution, and layer order, not browsing marketing content.

### Brand Personality
Practical, maker-focused, and precise. The app should feel like a capable workshop instrument: fast to understand, responsive under the hand, and visually grounded in the Minecraft subject matter without becoming toy-like.

### Aesthetic Direction
A dense utility webapp with a large first-screen 3D viewport, crisp side controls, earthy block-inspired accents, and high-contrast readable surfaces. The experience should avoid generic neon developer styling and avoid decorative landing-page sections.

### Design Principles
- Put the uploaded structure at the center of the first viewport.
- Keep controls compact, predictable, and reachable while inspecting.
- Use color to communicate block/material variety, layer position, and active state.
- Make empty and error states actionable without long instructions.
- Prefer performance and clarity over ornamental UI.
